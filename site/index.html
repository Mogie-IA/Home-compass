The problem

The home handover process is still too fragmented.

A new home comes with important information, but that information often gets spread across emails, PDFs, printed manuals, folders, WhatsApp messages, and different teams.

For developers

The handover pack becomes an operations problem.

  • Handover packs are difficult to organise consistently across homes.
  • Buyers often ask the same post-handover questions repeatedly.
  • Documents, warranties, certificates, and manuals can be hard to update after move-in.
  • Aftercare teams spend time searching for information that should already be easy to access.
  • The buyer experience after completion can feel less polished than the sales experience before completion.
For homeowners

The information a home needs goes missing.

  • Important documents get lost or buried in emails.
  • Appliance manuals and warranties are hard to find when needed.
  • Repairs, maintenance history, and key contacts are not stored in one place.
  • Homeowners may not know what is covered, who to contact, or when something needs attention.
  • When selling or renting later, property records are difficult to gather again.

Home Compass solves this by giving every property a structured digital record from day one.

The solution

One organised home profile. Built at handover. Useful for years.

Home Compass creates a digital record for each home. Developers can upload the key documents and handover information, while homeowners get simple access to everything they need after moving in.

The result is a more professional handover process, fewer repeated questions, and a better long-term homeowner experience.

The process

How Home Compass works

1

Developer creates the property record

The developer or estate team creates a digital profile for each property, including the address, plot number, development name, and basic handover details.

2

Documents and records are uploaded

Handover packs, warranties, manuals, certificates, appliance guides, utility information, and key contacts are added to the property record.

3

Homeowner gets access to My Home

The homeowner receives access to a clean dashboard where they can view documents, track key information, and find what they need after move-in.

4

The record stays useful after handover

Over time, the homeowner can use Home Compass to store repairs, reminders, updates, maintenance notes, and future property information.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

Everything you might want to know about the platform, the pilot, and what comes next. Something missing? Get in touch.

What is Home Compass?

Home Compass is a digital home record platform. It helps developers organise and deliver home handover information, and gives homeowners one place to access documents, warranties, manuals, repairs, reminders, and key property information.

Who is Home Compass for?

Home Compass is built for residential developers, estate teams, aftercare teams, and homeowners.

Is Home Compass only for new-build homes?

The first focus is new-build handover, because that is where important property information is created and transferred. Over time, Home Compass can also support existing homeowners who want to organise their home records.

What kind of documents can be stored?

Home Compass can store handover packs, warranties, certificates, manuals, appliance guides, property information, service records, repair notes, and key contacts.

Does Home Compass replace the developer’s aftercare team?

No. Home Compass supports aftercare by making information easier to access. It does not replace human support where support is needed.

Can homeowners add their own records?

Yes, the goal is for homeowners to keep building their home record over time with repairs, reminders, updates, and maintenance information.

Is this available now?

Home Compass is currently being prepared for pilot use. Developers can request a pilot, and homeowners can join the waitlist.

Can Home Compass be used for a small pilot?

Yes. A pilot can start with one development, one building, a small group of homes, or a selected handover phase.
