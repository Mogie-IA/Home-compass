# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Residential property developers, estate and aftercare teams evaluating a handover tool (primary CTA: request a pilot), and homeowners/new-build buyers who want their home documents organised (secondary CTA: join the waitlist). Developers arrive sceptical and time-poor; homeowners arrive frustrated by scattered paperwork.

## Product Purpose

Home Compass is a digital home handover and property record platform. The site's job is to convert developers into pilot requests and homeowners into waitlist signups by making the product feel organised, trustworthy, and modern — the qualities the product itself promises.

## Brand Personality

Organised, trustworthy, quietly modern. "Controlled complexity": sophisticated information made calm and manageable. Premium SaaS polish without hype — no inflated stats, no slogans.

## Anti-references

- Purple/violet/indigo-led AI-startup gradients (hard ban from project design standards).
- Gradient-filled headline words, meaningless stat rows, emoji in headings, pill-badge clutter, glassmorphism-by-default.
- "Why Choose Us?" interchangeable SaaS template copy.
- Anything that reads as a generated template rather than a designed site.

## Design Principles

1. Match the Clarasight-style reference layout, executed entirely in the brand's blue family — never purple.
2. The compass is the signature: cardinal-point diamonds, rings, and the rose motif carry identity; everything else stays quiet.
3. Show the product: real dashboard mockups in browser-chrome frames beat abstract illustration.
4. Copy is short and concrete; visuals carry the excitement.
5. Motion is intentional and physical (scroll-linked, spring-based), never decorative confetti.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast for body text; visible keyboard focus on all interactive elements; `prefers-reduced-motion` honoured on every animation (scroll-linked effects, marquees, ambient loops all degrade to static or instant); semantic HTML with labelled form controls and alt text on meaningful images.
